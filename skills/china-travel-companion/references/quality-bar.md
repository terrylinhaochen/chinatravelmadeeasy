# Quality bar

## Evidence

- Use primary sources for immigration, money limits, transport rules, registration, closures, and product capabilities.
- Use recent community reports to find failure modes, confusing interfaces, and on-the-ground workarounds.
- Record the checked date for changing claims.
- A recommendation without a source line or rationale is an idea, not a reviewed place.

## Place identity

A map-ready place has a canonical or commonly used English name, Chinese/local name when available, city or region, and enough source context to distinguish it from similarly named places. Coordinates are optional; invented coordinates are forbidden.

A complete China map handoff also names the intended execution surface and supplies the smallest reliable query for it. For chains, stations, hotels, attractions with multiple gates, and rural sights, city alone may be insufficient: preserve the verified branch, entrance, or Chinese address. Discovery provenance and the map query are separate fields.

Confidence meanings:

- `resolved`: first-party map/site or multiple reliable fields identify the same place.
- `probable`: name and city are plausible but need a map check.
- `unresolved`: missing city, ambiguous branch, vague neighborhood, or extraction noise.

## Shared task

The minimum complete shared-location story is:

1. ingest artifacts;
2. review candidates;
3. preserve source evidence;
4. resolve duplicates and bilingual identity;
5. collect group constraints and keep/maybe/drop decisions;
6. flag conflicts;
7. sequence kept places;
8. create a copyable or writable map handoff;
9. reopen the reviewed set later.

Do not call a local-only browser list “shared” unless another person can reopen a handoff. Do not call text copied to a clipboard a map export.

## Community contribution

- Private by default; contribution is explicit and revocable where the product supports accounts.
- Strip personal notes and split multi-city inputs before publishing.
- Require identity agreement before deduplication.
- Store evidence counts and correction history rather than converting popularity into truth.
- Measure resolution success, map handoffs, saves, corrections, and city coverage.

## Guide usefulness

Score each guide from 0 to 2 on:

- Decision clarity: says what to do for a defined traveler.
- Applicability: accounts for passport, device, route, city, timing, or budget.
- Failure recovery: gives independent fallbacks.
- Evidence: links current primary sources for changing claims.
- Recency: checked date matches the facts, not just an edited timestamp.
- Actionability: ordered steps, thresholds, addresses, or exact phrases.
- Honesty: names limits, city variation, and unresolved claims.

A publishable guide scores at least 11/14 with no zero in Evidence, Recency, or Honesty.
