This is the fastest three-city route I would still defend for a first trip: **Beijing (3 days) → Xi'an (1.5 days) → Shanghai (2.5 days)**, connected by high-speed rail. It can fit the [240-hour visa-free transit](/guides/china-visa-free-2026/) framework only when the traveler’s nationality, ports, permitted stay area, and A → China → different country or region C routing all qualify. An onward ticket alone does not prove eligibility.

## Before you go (the short version)

Two weeks out, run the [pre-departure checklist](/guides/china-pre-departure-checklist/). The itinerary-specific bookings, in order of how fast they sell out:

1. **Palace Museum / Forbidden City** — use the official international site and the exact passport you will carry. Book the morning slot for Day 2 as soon as the official window opens. If it is sold out, recent foreign-passport visitors report assisted purchase at the Meridian Gate service window, but build the trip as though that fallback may be unavailable.
2. **Trains** — sales open 15 days ahead ([trains guide](/guides/china-high-speed-trains/)). Book Beijing→Xi'an and Xi'an→Shanghai immediately for travel days 4 and 5.
3. **Mutianyu Great Wall transport** — choose the complete chain, not only a ticket: direct visitor bus, driver, or 916 Express to Huairou plus the official local-bus transfer. Save 慕田峪长城景区 rather than a tour-company pickup.
4. Hotels — see neighborhood picks below; confirm foreigner acceptance ([hotels guide](/guides/hotels-foreigners-china/)).

## Days 1–3: Beijing

**Stay**: near a Line 1/Line 8 metro station — Wangfujing or Dongcheng around the Drum Tower for atmosphere (hutong lanes), or a chain hotel near Beijing South station for convenience.

**Day 1 — land and warm up.** Clear immigration (transit-permit counter if applicable), eSIM on, cash from the ATM, airport express or Didi into town. Afternoon: **Temple of Heaven** park (locals dancing, tai chi, ¥35) — a gentle jet-lag sight. Evening: Peking duck; the famous chains (Siji Minfu, Quanjude) take walk-ins early — put your name in via the ticket machine and point.

**Day 2 — the imperial axis.** Morning slot at the **Palace Museum / Forbidden City**. The public museum entrance is Meridian Gate on the south side; Tian'anmen Square is nearby but has its own real-name reservation and security workflow, so do not discover that dependency at the checkpoint. Plan 3–4 hours inside, exit north, and climb **Jingshan Park** for the rooftop view of everything you just walked. If the palace booking fails, use Jingshan, Beihai, and a defined hutong walk rather than buying an unverified ticket from a tout.

**Day 3 — the Great Wall.** Give **Mutianyu** the whole day. Leave early, but budget according to the transport you actually booked: a direct vehicle and the official 916 Express plus Huairou transfer are not the same journey. At the scenic area, the shuttle, cable-car products, chairlift, and toboggan are separate decisions. Walk the ridge first; choose the descent only after weather, queues, and mobility are clear. Jinshanling is a different, more demanding excursion—not a same-day pin swap.

## Day 4: Train to Xi'an + evening city

Morning G train, Beijing West → Xi'an North, ~4.5 hours. Arrive 45+ minutes before departure; stations are airport-sized ([what to expect](/guides/china-high-speed-trains/)).

**Stay** inside the city walls near the Bell Tower. Afternoon: the **Muslim Quarter** food streets — this is a *grazing dinner*: roujiamo (Chinese "burger"), biangbiang noodles, lamb skewers, persimmon cakes. Evening: the city wall near South Gate is lit beautifully; rent a bike on top of the wall (14km loop) if you have the legs.

## Day 5: Terracotta Army → night train city or evening train to Shanghai

Morning: **Terracotta Warriors** (use the museum's official real-name reservation with the visitor's passport; only use a third-party product after confirming that exact admission is included). It's ~1 hour northeast — pre-book a driver, take the tourist bus from Xi'an North, or Didi it. See pits in order 1 → 3 → 2, ~2.5 hours; consider a licensed English guide at the entrance — context transforms it. Keep the full map identity, **秦始皇帝陵博物院**, rather than relying on an English “Terracotta Army” result.

Late afternoon: G train Xi'an North → Shanghai Hongqiao (~6 hours, arrive late) — or, if 6 evening hours on a train sounds grim, fly (2.5h + airport overhead, similar door-to-door). The train is easier logistics; bring snacks and watch China scroll past.

## Days 6–7: Shanghai

**Stay**: Jing'an (metro-connected, leafy, great food) or near East Nanjing Road for first-timer geography.

**Day 6 — the classic loop.** Morning: **Yu Garden** and the old town (go at opening, 8:30am, before the crush). Midday: walk the **former French Concession** — plane-tree streets around Wukang Road, coffee everywhere. Evening: the **Bund** at dusk as the Pudong skyline lights up — the single best free sight in China. Optional: ascend Shanghai Tower's observation deck (book a clear-weather slot same-day).

**Day 7 — pick your Shanghai.** For the museum version, Shanghai Museum East currently admits individual visitors without advance reservation, but closes Tuesday and stops entry before closing time; recheck before travel. A half-day at **Zhujiajiao** is possible, but with only two and a half Shanghai days it should replace a city theme, not be added on top. Depart from the exact airport and terminal on the ticket; for transit-rule itineraries, recheck that the onward destination makes the A → China → C routing valid.

## Daily-rhythm notes that save half-days

- **Classify the attraction before booking it.** For identity-bound reservations, enter each traveler's passport details exactly; at reservation-free or on-site venues, still carry the original passport. The [attraction ticket guide](/guides/china-attraction-tickets-reservations/) separates advance admission, free real-name reservations, layered products, and current walk-in entry instead of pretending every sight has one workflow.
- **Many museums and paid interiors close on Mondays**, including the Palace Museum except on some public holidays; parks and outdoor grounds may remain open while interior halls close. Verify each venue and schedule the Wall, parks, or hutong walking then.
- Chinese tourists travel early; sights are emptiest at opening and after 3pm.
- Metro + Didi covers everything in all three cities ([getting around](/guides/didi-metro-getting-around/)); never bother with a rental car.
- Eat dinner before 8:30pm outside big-city centers — kitchens close earlier than you'd think.
- Build one empty evening into the week. The best China memories are unplanned: a park dance circle, a chess game you get pulled into, a tea shop conversation via translation app.

This route is deliberately conservative — three cities in seven days is the right amount of motion for a first trip. Resist adding a fourth; come back instead. Everyone does.
